About BAMRU

The Bay Area Mountain Rescue Unit is an all-volunteer wilderness search and rescue (SAR) unit, based in the San Francisco Bay Area. We search for and assist people who are lost or stranded anywhere outdoors in California.

BAMRU is an independent, 501(c)(3) non-profit organization (Federal ID 23-7098709) with over 45 years of service to our community. BAMRU members include experienced outdoor enthusiasts and medical and outdoor professionals, and the diversity of our membership allows us to cross train and work safely and comfortably in challenging and austere wilderness situations.

Anytime, Anywhere, All Conditions

BAMRU is field deployable for up to 72 hours for anyone in harm’s way in all terrain, all weather and all seasons.

Operations range from searches for missing or lost individuals in the high Sierra to communities affected by wildfires. In addition to mountains, we search along coastal cliffs and in other rural and wilderness environments.

Our team is composed of rock climbers, mountaineers, backpackers and backcountry skiers. Our members are skilled to operate efficiently and safely in technical terrain on rock, snow and ice.

BAMRU is an accredited member of the Mountain Rescue Association and a FEMA and California OES Type I search and rescue resource. We are activated for operations by the San Mateo County Sheriff’s Office.

Get Involved

BAMRU serves our community by searching for those lost or injured in the wilderness. The team consists of volunteers who train for hundreds of hours each year to operate under stressful and rapidly changing weather and circumstances. While each member does not attend every operation, being on the team requires a significant time commitment and some flexibility with job and family responsibilities.

If you are interested in joining the team, attend one of our meetings, which are open to guests.
