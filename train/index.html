BAMRU Trainings

BAMRU members are continually improving and practicing their skills. Some trainings are open to guests who have attended the general meeting preceding the training; others are open only to BAMRU trainees and members. See the descriptions of individual events for further information.

Featured Training Events

Hover over the images to see the description for that training event. Some trainings are guest friendly!

  • Wilderness Search

    Mock Search and SAR basic are designed to show guests what it's like to respond to a callout and execute a search. (Guest-friendly!)

  • Navigation

    Navigation is more difficult than most are willing to admit. It's a perishable skill that requires constant practice.

  • Personal Rock Skills

    Without any outdoor rock skills, you cannot safely participate in searches that are often on steep, difficult terrain.

  • Tracking

    Tracking and clue-awareness skills prove highly valuable when searching for a lost person. (Guest-friendly!)

  • Technical Rope Rescue

    BAMRU trains to operate the twin tensioned-rope system (TTRS) for low and high-angle systems for rescuing patients.

  • Snow and Alpine

    We often operate in avalanche terrain, on steep snow and ice, and at altitude where the cold and exposure are unforgiving.

What trainings can I come to?

BAMRU offers search and rescue basic training (Mock SAR, and SAR Basic) that are guest-friendly. Attending these trainings and general meetings is the best way to learn about BAMRU, meet BAMRU members, acquire useful search-and-rescue skills, and prepare yourself to apply to join the Unit. Tracking, Medical, and Snow camping are also guest-friendly trainings to attend. Be sure to come to the general meeting preceding these trainings.

When and where does BAMRU train?

BAMRU operates anywhere in California. We typically train in our backyard, the Santa Cruz mountains, or in the Sierras. Trainings occur year round and follow a progression of skills, typically lasting entire weekends. Many trainings for gear and team familiarization (working with the rescue trucks, radio communication) occur locally.
